SRF-SVB: Style-Consistent Singing Voice Beautifying
via Rectified Flow

[SRF-SVB-DEMO]

Abstract

Singing voice beautifying (SVB) aims to correct pitch and rhythm while enhancing vocal quality, preserving lyrics and the singer's timbre. Existing methods suffer from limited correction scope, dependency on parallel data, difficulty in preserving the original singer's style, and inefficient inference. We propose SRF-SVB, a style-consistent model for SVB via rectified flow. To our knowledge, this is the first work to introduce rectified flow into the SVB task. We design a masked mel-spectrogram inpainting mechanism, where the model generates masked regions guided by spectral context and decoupled acoustic features including pitch, timbre, and content. During inference, the beautified singing is generated conditioned on the amateur spectral context, preserving the original singer's timbre characteristic and expressive patterns. Experiments show that SRF-SVB outperforms baseline methods in both objective and subjective metrics.
